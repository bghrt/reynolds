Exercises

 

Value: 1
Which is not a criteria for evaluating a resource? (Choose one)
    a.authority
    b.timeliness
    c.design/layout
    d.purpose/bias
    e.review process

 

Value: 1
How can you check for an author's credentials or expertise on a subject? (Choose one)
    a.Check a biographical source.
    b.Read a critical review.
    c.Search for other publications the author has written.
    d.Find out what institution or university the author is affiliated with.
    e.All of the above.

 

Value: 1
In fields such as history and literature, older materials may be just as valuable as newer ones.
    True
    False

 

Value: 1
All web sites have a similar purpose.
    True
    False

 

Value: 1
How can you find out if a journal is peer-reviewed? (Choose one)
    a.Check the title in the print or online version of Ulrich's Periodical Directory.
    b.Review the front pages of the print journal or the journal web page.
    c.Limit your search to peer reviewed journals using EBSCOhost databases such as Academic Search Complete.
    d.All of the above.

 

Value: 1
It is okay to cite a Wikipedia article in your research paper.
    True
    False
