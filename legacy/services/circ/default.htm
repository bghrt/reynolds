Circulation Policies & Procedures

Patron Eligibility and Registration:

  • Your Reynolds student/faculty ID card entitles you to check out physical circulating material from Reynolds Libraries.  Community users may obtain a library card with valid photo ID.  Material checked out on your card is your responsibility
  • All material borrowed from the Library must be checked out at the Circulation Desk or at a self-checkout station located Downtown and at Parham Road.  Ebooks may be checked out with your myReynolds login.
  • Patrons may obtain material from any Reynolds campus library either in person or through intra-campus loan. Requests normally arrive within 72 hours. Material can be returned to any Reynolds library.

Check-Out Policies

Material that can be checked out of the libraries include:

  • Books – 28 day checkout with one renewal – limit 15 items to Reynolds students, faculty and staff; 5 items to community members
  • Audio books – 28 day checkout with one renewal – limit 2 items
  • Popular DVDs – one week checkout with one renewal – limit 2 items

Please note: Renewal is permitted online, in-person or by telephone on or before the due date unless another patron has requested the item. If the item is past due, you must bring the item to the library and check it out in person again at the Circulation Desk. 

Reference books and periodicals may NOT be checked out.

Renewal and Holds

Books may be renewed in person, online, or by telephone during library hours. Books that are overdue or have a hold on them cannot be renewed. Popular DVDs cannot be renewed.

Holds may be placed on all circulating books that are currently checked out or are located at another Reynolds campus library. You must have a Reynolds student ID to place holds.

Delinquent Materials:

Overdue materials that have not been returned will result in a block being placed on the patron's record. Patrons may not check out materials, obtain transcripts or register for classes until the block is cleared. Library blocks will be cleared when the items are returned and/or when the fee is paid.

Patrons who are late in returning DVDs will be charged a $1.00 per day per item fine.

Items more than 30 days overdue are considered delinquent items and a billing letter will be sent. The charge for a delinquent or damaged item is:

  • $65 per book or the replacement cost of the book, whichever is greater
  • $50 per DVD or Audiobook or the replacement cost, whichever is greater

Patrons may be issued a refund for overdue items that they have paid for and are subsequently able to return. Material must be:

  • Undamaged
  • Returned within 6 months of the original due date of the material
  • Not yet replaced

Reynolds Library does not accept patron-purchased replacement copies of lost material in lieu of payment of the replacement fee.
The replacement fee for lost or damaged Interlibrary Loan material will be determined by the respective lending library.
Unpaid library obligations will prevent students from registering and obtaining grade transcripts. College policy requires that debts not paid be sent to a collection agency at which time additional fees may accrue.

Intra-campus Loans:

Patrons may obtain material from any Reynolds campus library through intra-campus loan. Requests normally arrive within 72 hours.

Interlibrary Loan (ILL):

ILL service is available for materials not held by the library. ILL requests can be submitted online or in print (PDF). Only Reynolds faculty, staff, and students are eligible to borrow on interlibrary loan. Textbooks are not available through interlibrary loan. For further information please see the library's ILL policy (PDF).

Richmond Academic Library Cooperative:

The Richmond Academic Library Cooperative (RALC) provides limited reciprocal borrowing privileges for students, faculty and staff of its members. Borrowers must abide by all policies of the lending library, including loan periods, fines, and replacement charges. Faculty, staff and students must obtain a pass at the Reference Desk in order to borrow from another RALC institution.
