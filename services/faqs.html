FAQs for Reynolds Library

Use the drop down below to find an answer to your question. If the answer is not available below, please email us at library@reynolds.edu

Are food or drink allowed in the library?

Food and drink are allowed in Reynolds Library, but not at the library computers, per Reynolds policy 4-3. Library users who violate these restrictions will be asked to take their food or drink outside the library.

Back to top

Can non-Reynolds affiliates use library databases?

Non-Reynolds affiliates can only use the library electronic databases in the library. No off-campus access is available.

Back to top

Can I place an item on hold and have it sent to my home campus?

Holds may be placed on all circulating books that are currently checked out or are located at another Reynolds campus library. You must have a Reynolds student ID to place holds. To place an item on hold, start at the library catalog. Find the book you are searching for and then click on request this item.

 

 

 

Then make sure to select the pick up location for the book.

Back to top

Can I return library materials to any campus library?

Yes, you can return Reynolds library materials to any Reynolds campus library.

Back to top

Can students reserve study rooms?

Group study rooms may be used by Reynolds affiliates for academic and college-related activities. Read more about study room policies here.

Back to top

Do you have scanners?

Yes, self-serve photocopiers and copies also have scanning capabilities. You must have a jump drive on you, in order to scan documents. Scanning onto a jumpdrive is free.

Back to top

Does the library have a photocopier for public use?

Yes, self-serve photocopiers and copies are five cents. However, you must purchase an Equitrac card in order to make photocopies. Parham Campus (only) does have a coin operated photocopier. An alternate option might be to scan a copy onto your jump drive. This option is free.

Back to top

How do I get a library card to check out materials?

Students, faculty, and staff can check out materials using their Reynolds ID cards. Virginia state residents not affiliated with Reynolds must show their Driver’s License/State ID to apply for a community card at the circulation desk before checking out materials. Non-Reynolds individuals are the only ones that need a separate card.

Back to top

How many books can I check out at one time?

Students may have up to 15 items checked out at one time. Community patrons may check out up to five books. See Circulation Polices for more information.

Back to top

Does the library offer instruction on how to use library resources?

Yes, the library offers workshops for improving your research skills throughout the year. See the Workshops page for descriptions, schedules and registration.

Back to top

Does the library have a fax machine for student use?

No, the library does not have a fax machine for student use. However, the library has scanners that will allow any patron to save scanned documents to a USB drive. Once saved, the documents can be e-mailed using a library computer. Please seek a Reynolds librarian for help.

Back to top

How do I get materials from a different library?

Need an article not in the databases? Found the perfect book at VCU? Reynolds Library offers multiple options to obtain materials: Cooperative Borrowing, RALC Pass and Interlibrary Loan. These services are only available to current students, faculty and staff. Check If We Don?t Have It for more details.

Back to top

How do I access library databases from off-campus?

You can use your MyReynolds username and password to access library databases from off campus.

Back to top

How do I renew a book?

Books may be renewed online, or, during library hours, in person or by telephone. To renew online, sign into the Renew Books link (which is on the main library page in the search box) with your MyReynolds account. Click on loans number. The items you have checked out will be displayed. Click on the item you want to renew. If you books are not overdue, you are eligible for renewal. If it is eligible for renewal, you can click Renew. You will be taken back to your list of checked out items and the new due date will be displayed. If you cannot access your account, please call your library circulation desk: Downtown: 804.523.5211, Parham: 804.523.5220, Goochland: 804.523.5419. Ask the staff to check your campus location in your account.

Back to top

How much does printing cost?

In order to print, you must first purchase an Equitrac card for $1. Then you must add at least $1 value to the card. Printing is $.05 a side for black and white, and $.20 for color.

For the library hours, see our calendar page.

What is the loan period for library materials?

Books and Audiobooks: 4 weeks (28 days) with the possibility of one additional 4-week renewal. DVDs can be checked out for one week. We do renew DVDs for an additional week as long as they are not overdue. Some materials may have special loan periods. See Circulation Polices for more information.
