PDOs for Faculty

Below is our complete list of PDO workshop offerings. If you are interested in attending one or more of these workshops this semester, please let us know so we can schedule them accordingly via the Knowledge Center.

Below are a few links to presentations and guides we've created in conjunction with those PDOs that may be of interest to you.
• Beyond the Textbook: Integrating Library Resources into Your Blackboard Courses
•Library in your Pocket: Go Mobile @ Reynolds Library

Many of the workshops listed below, are also offered as open session workshops for students. You are welcome to attend these open session workshops as well.

Apps, eBooks, & Primo: 3 in 1 Library Workshop
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
This workshop will provide a quick overview and getting started with using: some of our favorite apps; our EBSCOhost and Safari eBook collections; and the Primo one-stop discovery tool for locating print and online resources.

Beyond the Textbook: Integrating Library Services into Blackboard
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Learn how to create direct links to library research guides, online databases, articles and books from your Blackboard course/s. Topics covered will include: Linking to library research guides and course guides; Off-campus access (proxy server information); Linking to articles (persistent links) from the following databases – EBSCOhost, Gale, JSTOR, and Ovid and Wiley; Linking to online books from the EBSCOhost and Safari databases; and copyright issues.

Cite it Right: APA
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Learn how to identify parts of a citation; how to cite a variety of print and online resources in APA style using: database citation tools; the WorldCat Cite this Item tool; and various online resource examples and citation generators.

Cite it Right: MLA
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Learn how to identify parts of a citation; how to cite a variety of print and online resources in MLA style using: database citation tools; the WorldCat Cite this Item tool; and various other online resource examples and citation generators.

EasyBib
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Learn how to create citations and a bibliography for your research paper easily and quickly using the EasyBib tool. This workshop will cover how to use EasyBib to: create citations in MLA, APA, or Chicago style using the Autocite and Manual entry forms; create annotations; create parenthetical citations or footnotes; import citations from library databases such as EBSCOhost or JSTOR; use the Notebook feature to take notes and link notes to sources; and export citations to MS Word or Google Docs to create a bibliography.

eBooks 101
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Let’s get digital! Get hands-on with the library’s ever-increasing collection of eBooks including Safari, EBSCOhost, SciVerse, OverDrive Audio, and more!

Google Apps for Education
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Explore the many tools available through your VCCS Google apps account: create, collaborate, share, and store documents, spreadsheets, and slides in the cloud via Google Drive; meet and collaborate with two or more people via Google Hangouts video chat service; create and share web pages via Google Sites; participate in online discussions via Google Groups; and share content and network with others via Google+.

How to Make Google Scholar Work for You
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Using Google Scholar often provides a mixed-bag of results; learn how to use Google Scholar combined with Library tools to find the articles you want. See how the results differ from using library databases, and discuss the pros and cons of using one over the other.

Introduction to Library Services (Component of ORNT-100: New Employee Orientation)
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
Brief introduction to the various resources and services available at Reynolds Libraries including library web site demonstration and distribution of handout.

Is this Web Site Credible?
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes Learn the specific criteria for evaluating web sites including: accuracy, authority, currency, objectivity, and coverage. A variety of web sites will be reviewed based on these evaluation criteria.

Library in your Pocket: Go Mobile @ Reynolds Library
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
Bought a cool new tablet but not sure how to use it for research? Bring your tablet or eReader in for a tour of the digital resources of Reynolds Library. No device yet? We have a few to available for test drive on the information highway—no license required!

Nursing & Health Databases: Getting Started
Audience: Faculty
Length: 60 minutes
An introduction to finding articles and other literature related to the field of nursing and allied health professions using databases such as CINAHL, Health Source, MEDLINE, and Nursing & Allied Health Collection.

Popular Databases: Getting Started
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
Audience: Faculty & Staff Length: 60 minutes An introduction to finding articles and other literature on various topics using our four most popular databases: EBSCOhost, Factiva, JSTOR, and LexisNexis Academic.

Preventing & Detecting Plagiarism
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
Learn specific strategies to help students avoid plagiarism by using sources appropriately in their research papers. Also learn various ways to detect plagiarism.

Research the Smart Way
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
An introduction to the services and resources available at Reynolds Library. Topics covered include sources for topic ideas and overviews, strategies for searching the library catalog and online databases, how to access licensed library databases from off-campus, how to obtain the full text of an article, and the differences between Internet and licensed library databases.

Technology Petting Zoo
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
Get up close and person with a variety of mobile devices at the Technology Petting Zoo. Catch a demo of Nook, Kindle and iPad. See how resources from Reynolds library, the public library, and open resources work with the devices. Get hands on experience with each when you test-drive the devices. We promise they won’t bite!

What the Heck is Web 2.0?
Audience: Faculty & Staff
Length: 60 minutes
This session will give a brief overview of some recent online trends, including social networking websites like LinkedIn and Facebook, Social Bookmarking, blogs, RSS feeds, and instant messaging. Learn how these trends are changing the way people obtain information, and get some tips to get started so you don’t feel left behind in the dust.
