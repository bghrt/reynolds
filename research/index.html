Research Portal

Easy Bib

A web-based tool for building and managing citations in MLA, APA and Chicago Style. No login is required for on-campus access. Use your MyReynolds login off-campus. (Citation Client)

Check it out here!

Gale Virtual Reference Library

This is a great place to start your research in order to get a thorough overview of your topic. It includes multi-volume encyclopedias, biographical collections, consumer health references, and specialized reference sources. Subjects covered include arts and humanities, biography and genealogy, business and industries, general and consumer interests, government and political science, health and medicine, law, science and technology, and social sciences.

Check it out!

JSTOR

This is a great resource for humanities and social science based research. If you're taking a history, philosophy, political science or literature class JSTOR offers excellent scholarly articles. JSTOR also offers great access to science resources. However, it doesn't provide the most recent content.

Check it out

What are library databases?

When the Internet started becoming really popular, companies started taking content that was originally published in print, putting that content online, and then selling that content to libraries. So, library databases are essentially print sources made available online. Our databases are the way to go for academic research. Something that has originally been published in print has been evaluated and edited before it has been published. The sources that you use from Google (About.com, Buzzle.com, Wikipedia, etc.) often have less depth are are more prone to falsehoods. The databases above are just a tiny sample of our database options. Use the Databases by Subject to find sources by discipline or topic area or use A to Z Databases to find specific databases. And if you get stuck, remember we're here to support you.

Opposing Viewpoints

If you're taking English 111/112 and writing a persuasive paper, this is a great resource. This database offers Pro/Con articles on the hottest social issues, from "fracking" to climate change, from health care reform to immigration, from gay marriage to violence in video games. If it's a controversial topic, you'll find and article about it here.

Check it out!

Films On Demand

If you need to show a video for class or incorporate one into your PowerPoint, check out Films on Demand. Choose from more than 20,000 educational titles in dozens of subject areas.

Check it out!

Academic Search Complete

If there is one database that librarians use the most, it's this one. What it comes down to is that there are more sources in Academic Search Complete than in nearly any other database. Start here for research in nearly any discipline or topic.

Check it out!
